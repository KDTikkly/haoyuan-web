# FGO Progression System — Deep Analysis

## Motivation

*Fate/Grand Order* is one of the highest-grossing mobile games globally. Its core competitive edge is not narrative or character design, but a **precisely engineered progression system** — combining an exceptionally low entry barrier with near-infinite depth, locking in a broad ACG audience for over a decade.

## Progression Layer Breakdown

### Layer 1: Character Acquisition (Gacha Funnel)
- Limited banner UP pools: artificially manufacture scarcity, amplify FOMO
- Pity system (CN server: 300 pulls): reduces sunk-cost anxiety, boosts payment decision confidence

### Layer 2: Enhancement System (NP Level × Skills × Grail)
- **Linear input, non-linear returns**: high marginal efficiency before NP5 incentivizes repeat spending
- Skill upgrade materials hard-bound to main quests → drives content consumption

### Layer 3: Craft Essence System
- Enhances rather than replaces servant power → extends per-servant spending cycle

## Lifecycle Management Strategy

| Phase | Core Strategy | Operational Tactic |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| Acquisition | Low barrier + generous giveaways | Anniversary / holiday free quartz campaigns |
| Retention | Daily tasks × main story updates | Chapter-driven narrative, creating a "seasonal anime" feel |
| Re-engagement | Rerun limiteds + event rallies | Classic banner reruns reactivate dormant players |
| Monetization | Limited × high-power servants | Super-tier limiteds launched at story climax moments |

## Insights & Conclusions

FGO's success is fundamentally a **triple resonance of emotional bonding × collection-completeness anxiety × community identity**. Compared to pure combat-value games, IP emotional investment significantly raises players' acceptance threshold for "high-value" purchases.

> **Key takeaway**: For new ACG mobile game projects, invest early in IP emotional foundation. Treat progression depth as a mid-to-late retention tool, not an early acquisition driver.
